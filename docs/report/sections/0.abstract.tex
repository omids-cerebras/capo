\abstract{
\textbf{Covariance-Aware Policy Optimization (CAPO)} is a hierarchical Bayesian gradient aggregation method for Reinforcement Learning with Verifiable Rewards (RLVR) that replaces token-independence assumptions with a principled, data-driven treatment of structured covariance. Starting from a generalized Bayesian working model over per-trajectory gradient statistics, we place a Jeffreys prior on the scale parameter and show that the resulting posterior-optimal correction recovers the $\Delta L$ normalization as a special case. CAPO then generalizes this view by estimating an empirical variance function that captures realistic within-trajectory dependencies (e.g., banded structure, geometrically decaying, or state-dependent correlations). Under these models, trajectory-level gradient variance grows approximately linearly with length, clarifying why naive length-proportional weighting can be unstable and motivating sublinear dampening $w(L)\propto L^{-\alpha}$ with $\alpha<1$ to control posterior risk and hedge variance-estimation noise. We provide consistent variance estimators from rollouts, a plug-in weighting rule with closed-form updates, and diagnostics for when CAPO reduces variance without introducing bias. Across synthetic and RLVR benchmarks, CAPO stabilizes training and accelerates wall-clock convergence relative to $\Delta L$ and token-independent baselines.

\textbf{Keywords}: Reinforcement Learning with Verifiable Rewards (RLVR); Bayesian Methods; Variance Reduction; Policy Optimization; Large Language Models; Sequence Modeling; Stochastic Optimization; Robust Training; Statistical Inference.

}
