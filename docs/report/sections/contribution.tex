\section*{Contributions}

\paragraph{Overview.}
We address gradient aggregation for Reinforcement Learning with Verifiable Rewards (RLVR) under highly variable response lengths and realistic token dependencies. Our approach, \emph{Covariance-Aware Policy Optimization (CAPO)}, unifies prior length-based heuristics under a Bayesian perspective and introduces a simple, plug-in rule that yields unbiased, lower-variance updates without relying on token-independence assumptions.

\paragraph{Key contributions.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Unified analysis.} We place existing length-normalization rules (including $\Delta L$) on a coherent Bayesian footing via scale-invariant reasoning, clarifying the invariances and assumptions behind them.
  \item \textbf{CAPO aggregator.} We generalize beyond independence by estimating per-trajectory variance from short-range covariance structure and combining it with sublinear length dampening (\(\beta<1\)) to hedge estimation noise.
  \item \textbf{Theory to practice.} We show that trajectory-level gradient variance grows approximately linearly with length under broad dependency classes, motivating CAPO’s form and its guidance for choosing $\beta<1$.
  \item \textbf{Simple implementation.} CAPO is a drop-in change: variance estimates come from lightweight covariance summaries; weights are computed in a few lines and add negligible overhead.
  \item \textbf{Diagnostics and robustness.} We provide practical checks (effective sample size, weight entropy, CV ratios, residual autocorrelation) to tune hyperparameters and detect covariance misfit.
  \item \textbf{ICML-feasible empirical protocol.} We specify a minimal, controlled evaluation suite (\S\ref{sec:results}) designed to be runnable within approximately one week: it compares CAPO against GRPO/DAPO/Dr.~GRPO and $\Delta L$ under matched tokens, reports both end accuracy and stability diagnostics, and includes targeted ablations (sublinear exponent and covariance window size) to isolate covariance-aware effects.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Takeaway.}
CAPO offers a principled, covariance-aware alternative to length-only normalization: it preserves unbiasedness, reduces variance, and delivers stable, efficient RLVR training with minimal engineering effort.
